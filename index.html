About me

I am a Ph.D. student at ÉTS - École de technologie supérieure (Montreal - Canada) since May-2015. My research is focused on Offline Signature Verification Systems, in particular on learning feature representations from handwritten signature images.

I obtained my master's degree in Computer Science from the Federal University of Paraná (Curitiba / Brazil) with focus in Machine Learning. My research consisted in exploring Deep Learning models for Texture Classification (see the Publications tab for published papers and the thesis).

I have also worked as a software engineer at ExxonMobil, having solid programming skills in .NET development (C#), Microsoft SQL Server development (T-SQL) and administration.

Research Interests

I am interested in Machine Learning theory and applications, with a focus in Computer Vision tasks. In particular, I am interested in methods that can explore large datasets and learn directly from "raw" data (i.e. without the usage of feature extractors).

Some topics of my interest:

  • Computer vision applications
  • Feature Learning / Representation Learning
  • Convolutional Neural Networks
  • Data visualization techniques (such as t-SNE)
  • Adversarial examples
