About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at ÉTS - École de technologie supérieure. I have been modelling and training deep learning systems for computer vision for the past 5 years, with 7 published articles.

My area of research is machine learning applied to computer vision, and I am currently working on the task of Offline Handwritten Signature Verification. I also have a master's in Computer Science, in which I explored Deep Learning models for Texture Classification. I have 5 years of experience in the academia, defining research objectives, modeling problems to be solved using machine learning, training models such as convolutional neural networks, performing error analysis, among other competences. I participated in the 2017 NIPS competition on adversarial attacks/defenses, obtaining two silver medals and one bronze medal (the competition had 3 tracks). I also participated in two editions of the Deep Learning Summer School organized by the MILA lab (in 2015 and 2016).

Besides my experience in Machine Learning, I had 5 years of experience in the industry, working as a software engineer and Database administrator at ExxonMobil.
